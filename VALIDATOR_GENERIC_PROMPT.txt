================================================================================
VALIDATOR PROMPT - GENERIC RULES WITH MULTIPLE EXAMPLES
================================================================================

LOCATION: training/local_model_server.py
ENDPOINT: POST /validate-products

================================================================================
THE PROBLEM WITH DOMAIN-SPECIFIC EXAMPLES
================================================================================

PREVIOUS APPROACH (Golf-heavy):
--------------------------------
Example (Requested: golf club):
  Golf club set → RELEVANT
  Golf driver → RELEVANT
  Golf stand bag → RELATED (storage FOR clubs)
  Golf glove → RELATED (worn WHILE using clubs)
  Golf umbrella → RELATED (used ON golf course)
  Tennis racket → NOT_RELEVANT

ISSUES:
❌ Too golf-specific
❌ Model may overfit to golf accessories
❌ May not generalize well to other sports/products
❌ Heavy on golf, light on general rules

RESULT:
When validating "tennis racket", model might be confused because it learned
too much about golf and not enough about the general pattern.

================================================================================
THE SOLUTION: GENERIC RULES + MULTIPLE TINY EXAMPLES
================================================================================

NEW APPROACH:
-------------
Decision Rules:
RELEVANT: The candidate IS the requested product type.
RELATED: The candidate is used WITH, carries, stores, protects, or complements 
         the requested product, but is NOT the requested product itself.
NOT_RELEVANT: The candidate belongs to a different product type.

Examples:
Requested: backpack
  Backpack → RELEVANT
  Rain cover for backpack → RELATED

Requested: football
  Football → RELEVANT
  Football pump → RELATED

Requested: golf club
  Golf club → RELEVANT
  Golf bag → RELATED
  Tennis racket → NOT_RELEVANT

Requested: bottle
  Water bottle → RELEVANT
  Bottle cage → RELATED
  Backpack → NOT_RELEVANT

BENEFITS:
✅ Generic rule that applies to ALL product types
✅ Multiple domains (backpack, football, golf, bottle)
✅ Shows pattern across different categories
✅ Negative examples make boundaries clear
✅ Each example is tiny (1-2 lines)
✅ Model learns the general principle, not just golf

================================================================================
WHY MULTIPLE TINY EXAMPLES WORK BETTER
================================================================================

ONE DETAILED EXAMPLE:
Teaches specifics about one domain.
Model learns: "golf bags are RELATED to golf clubs"
May not generalize.

MULTIPLE TINY EXAMPLES:
Teaches the general pattern.
Model learns: "storage/accessory items are RELATED to the main product"
Generalizes to any product type.

ANALOGY:
Bad teaching: "An apple is red, round, and sweet. Here are 10 facts about apples."
Good teaching: "An apple is a fruit. An orange is a fruit. A banana is a fruit."

The student learns "what makes something a fruit" rather than "what an apple is."

================================================================================
THE POWER OF NEGATIVE EXAMPLES
================================================================================

WHY NEGATIVES MATTER:
LLMs learn boundaries just as much from what something is NOT.

Example WITHOUT negative:
  Requested: golf club
  Golf club → RELEVANT
  Golf bag → RELATED

The model knows golf club is RELEVANT and golf bag is RELATED.
But what about golf glove? Golf umbrella? Tennis racket?

Example WITH negative:
  Requested: golf club
  Golf club → RELEVANT
  Golf bag → RELATED
  Tennis racket → NOT_RELEVANT

Now the model learns:
- Golf equipment (club, bag) = same domain
- Tennis equipment = different domain
- NOT_RELEVANT means "completely different sport/category"

TWO NEGATIVE EXAMPLES:
  golf club → ... → Tennis racket → NOT_RELEVANT
  bottle → ... → Backpack → NOT_RELEVANT

Teaches: NOT_RELEVANT = different product category entirely, not just accessories.

RESPONSIBILITIES:
1. Hybrid Search: Find EVERYTHING related to query (broad)
2. Validator: Separate primary products from accessories (precise)
3. Frontend: Show RELEVANT first, RELATED separately

Clean separation!

================================================================================
WHY THIS DESIGN WORKS
================================================================================

HYBRID SEARCH ROLE:
- Cast a wide net
- Find all products related to "golf club"
- Includes: clubs, bags, gloves, umbrellas, tees
- Uses keyword + semantic matching
- Should be BROAD

VALIDATOR ROLE:
- Precise classification
- Separate "golf club" (RELEVANT) from "golf bag" (RELATED)
- Apply learned rules generically
- Should be PRECISE

If we filtered at search time:
❌ Miss accessories users might want
❌ Reduce product discovery
❌ Search becomes too narrow

By filtering at validation time:
✅ Find everything relevant
✅ Classify precisely
✅ Frontend has flexibility
✅ Better user experience
